What is IB?

"International Baccalaureate offers an education for students from age 3 to 19, of four programmes, that focus on teaching students to think critically and independently, and how to inquire with care and logic. The IB prepares students to succeed in a world where facts and fiction merge in the news, and where asking the right questions is a crucial skill that will allow them to flourish long after they’ve left our programmes. We are supported by IB teachers and coordinators who develop and promote the IB’s curriculums in almost 5,000 schools globally every day, in over 150 countries around the world."

www.ibo.org

Why should you be in IB in highschool?

With a 77% pass rate in a May 2019 report, an IB diploma shows that not only you pass highschool classes, but actually went into depth of those classes and have a commitment to higher education. This looks very good on college applications, since it shows that you're mature, hardworking, and organize. In 2018, an IB Student Acceptance Rates report shows that Princeton University, one of the top universities, had a 6.5% acceptance rate, but 17.2% of those people were IB students. Also, since small amount of people are in IB, it stands out in college applications.

Sources:IBO Data 2019 and IB Student Acceptance Rate 2018

How do I complete my IB diploma?


During Junior and Senior year, you must have taken classes of DP core and in the six subjects group. The DP core is theory of knowledge, extended essay, and community serivce to "aims to broaden students’ educational experience and challenge them to apply their knowledge and skills." Also, you can choose courses from six different groups:studies language and literature; language acquisition; individuals and societies; sciences; mathematics; and the arts. You can exchange arts for additional science, individuals and societies, or language course. You will have to take atleast 3 subjects higher level and the remaining at standard level, usually pick the classes you are strong at.
